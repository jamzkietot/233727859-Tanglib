Safety Tips and Guidelines

Stay safe while exploring Baguio City with these helpful tips.

← Back to Home

General Safety & Personal Security

  • Be Aware of Surroundings: Petty theft like pickpocketing can occur in crowded areas such as Session Road, public markets, and jeepneys. Keep your belongings secure (use zipped bags, avoid displaying expensive gadgets).
  • Nightlife Safety: Go out with friends at night. Avoid walking alone in dimly lit or isolated areas like certain parts of Burnham Park or Camp John Hay. Use taxis or ride-hailing services like Grab.
  • Use Reliable Transportation: When using taxis, ensure the driver uses the meter. Avoid unregistered vehicles and consider ride-hailing apps for convenience and safety.
  • Stay Aware of Scams: Be cautious of overpricing by vendors or taxi drivers. Decline help from strangers asking for money and watch out for online rental scams.

Compliance with Local Ordinances

  • Traffic Rules: "King of the Road" Ordinance requires stopping for five seconds before pedestrian lanes. Max speed limits are 20–30 kph in various areas.
  • Anti-Distracted Walking: Using mobile devices or reading while walking/crossing streets is prohibited.
  • Public Order:
    • No Smoking in public places.
    • No Burning of garbage or bonfires outside residences.
    • Silent Night Ordinance: Avoid excessive loud sounds in residential areas and public streets during certain hours.
    • Curfew for Minors: 9:00 PM – 4:00 AM.
